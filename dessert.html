    This weeeks feature dessert is.......CHOCOLATE MOUSSE! These treats are an essential after a long week!

    Ingridients (4 serves)

    • 3 eggs
    • 125g dark chocolate 70% cocoa
    • 10g unsalted butter
    • 1/2 cup cream, full fat
    • 3 tbsp caster sugar
    • Whipped cream
    • Chocolate shavings

    Recipe

    1. Separate eggs and yolks while eggs are cold. Place whites in a large bowl and yolks in a small bowl. Leave whites while you prepare other ingredients.
    2. Whisk yolks and place chocolate and butter in a bowl to melt in the microwave in 30-second bursts until smooth.
    3. Beat cream until stiff peaks form then add sugar. Beat whites until firm peaks form and fold egg yolks into the cream using a rubber spatula.
    4. Pour chocolate into cream yolk mixture and add 1/4 of beaten egg whites into chocolate mixture. Fold through until incorporated.
    5. Pour chocolate mixture into egg whites. Fold through until incorporated and no more white lumps remain.
    6. Divide mixture between 4 small glasses or pots. Refrigerate for at least 5 hours, preferably overnight.
    7. To serve, garnish with cream and chocolate shavings.

    Tips

    It is not possible to achieve the same result without using eggs, despite what other recipes promise - and I've tried many. It will either be denser, or be like eating whipped cream, or have a weird jelly like texture more like pana cotta.
